Theory

According to Rutherford’s model, an atom consists of a small, dense, positively charged nucleus, around which electrons revolve in circular orbits, similar to planets around the Sun. Most of the atom’s volume is empty space. The model was supported by the famous gold-foil experiment.

Conclusion from Experiment

  • Most α-particles passed through without deflection → most of the atom is empty space.
  • A small fraction were deflected at large angles → nucleus is small, dense, and positively charged.
  • Large deflections occur when α-particles approach the nucleus closely.

Limitations

  • Could not explain stability of atoms — why orbiting electrons don’t spiral into the nucleus.
  • Could not account for discrete spectral lines of hydrogen and other elements.
  • Neglected quantum mechanical effects.
